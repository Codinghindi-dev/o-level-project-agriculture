Over the centuries, farming has evolved from simple hand tools to advanced machinery that helps farmers increase productivity and reduce manual labor. Agricultural tools are instruments or machines used by farmers to perform various agricultural operations efficiently. These tools make farming easier, faster, and more productive. Depending on the purpose, agricultural tools can be simple hand tools or large mechanized machines.

Some common agricultural operations that require tools include:

  • Ploughing and tilling the land
  • Sowing and planting seeds
  • Irrigation and watering
  • Applying fertilizers and pesticides
  • Harvesting crops
  • Storing and transporting produce

Agricultural tools can be broadly divided into two categories:

1. Traditional Tools

Simple, manually operated tools used for centuries. Examples: plough, sickle, hoe, spade. Still common in small-scale & subsistence farming.

2. Modern Tools

Machine-powered tools for scale and precision. Examples: tractors, seed drills, rotavators, combine harvesters.
